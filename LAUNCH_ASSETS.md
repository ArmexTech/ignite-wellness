## Day 1 launch plan — 90 minutes, start here

Do these five things in order, on the same day. This is the single highest-leverage Day 1:

1. **Text 10 people** who'd genuinely want this (see *Personal text* below). Warmest audience, fastest signups, and these ones refer friends who then refer friends. The referral loop starts here.
2. **Post to your personal Instagram / Facebook / LinkedIn** using the launch post below. If you've got a story feature, do a Story too — it converts higher than the feed.
3. **Post to one Reddit community** you're a member of (not a spam). See the Reddit section for picked subs + template.
4. **Send the email** at the bottom to your broader friend group (or a BCC email to ~30-50 people you'd be fine hearing from).
5. **Reply to every signup** in the admin dashboard personally for the first week. Set up a saved reply ("hey — thanks for being early, here's what's coming next...") — feels human at low volume, builds loyalty later.

If you get ~20-50 signups from Day 1, you're on track. Those 20-50 become your first referral layer.

## Answers to questions people will ask

**"Is this just another fitness app?"**
> It's built from scratch for women specifically. Cycle-aware programming, postnatal and perimenopause tracks built with women's-health physios, 15-minute minimum-effective-dose workouts. If that sounds like marketing — take the quiz and try the demo, the personalization is real.

**"When will it launch?"**
> Soon. The waitlist lets me build the content library properly before opening the doors — plus gives early joiners the founding-member discount.

**"How much is it?"**
> $19.99/month, $39.99 quarterly ($13.33/mo), or $99.99/year ($8.33/mo). Waitlist locks in 50% off the first year.

**"Why cycle-aware matters?"**
> Because your estrogen and progesterone swing by 20x across a month and that changes how your body responds to training. Ignoring it means under-recovering half the month.

**"I'm on birth control / don't have a cycle — does this work?"**
> Yes — if you don't have a natural cycle, training stays consistent week-to-week and we track energy/sleep instead. The cycle-aware logic is optional, not required.

**"Will it work for postnatal?"**
> There's a dedicated postnatal track built with women's-health physios. It starts with inner-core rehab and progresses over 6 months. Read the [guide](https://ignite-wellness-production.up.railway.app/guide-postnatal.html) for the full framework.

**"What if I've been at this for years and don't need the basics?"**
> The plan adapts to your level. Same app can run a 60-year-old beginner or an intermediate lifter with 10 years in the gym — different progression, same logic.
